About me

Sarah Oelker has 20+ years of experience in academic libraries as a science librarian, instructional technologist, and data services specialist, most of it in small liberal arts colleges. They have extensive experience in the research data librarianship community, with presentations, papers, and collaborations across multiple types of institutions. They are committed to helping researchers solve the problems that keep them awake at night regarding research data storage, retention, processing pipelines, and training of students in data handling skills.

Sarah is a certified Carpentries instructor and has been an executive board member of the New England Software Carpentry Library Consortium (NESCLiC). They have taught a variety of learners essential scientific programming, data cleanup, and related computing skills, and they continue to broaden their own data science skillset in support of their consulting and teaching.
